Physics

  • *Self-Consistent calculation with adaptive boundary condition for electrons in Silicon Nanostructure.

This project was done in my undergraduate year with great help from Dr G. Gopir. It involves computing the eigenfunction and eigenenergies of electrons in the inversion layer in an n-MOSFET's gate, by solving the Schrodinger and Maxwell equations itteratively. Stable solutions were found with suitable initial guess. The algorithm was further stablized by considering an adaptive boundary condition. The results was subsequently presented in the Regional Conference of Solid State Science and Technology (RCSSST 2001), and it was published in Solid State Science and Technology 20:88-95.

  • *Controlling the repulsive Casimir force with optical Kerr effect.

This project was done in my postgraduate year along with great help from C.H. Raymond. Ooi. It seeks a theoretical method to control the Casimir force between two parallel plate by varying the intensity of impinging laser. With a good combination of materials, we can see that the Casimir force may be varied between attractive and repulsive, just by changing the intensity of laser! The results were published in Phys. Rev. A 86:062509. This project was also presented in the National Conference on Physics (PERFIK 2012), with several results subsequently published in Sains Malaysiana 42(12)(2013): 1799–1803.
