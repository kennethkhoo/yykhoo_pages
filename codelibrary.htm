Quantative Finance

  • Object oriented Monte Carlo framework using Parallel - C#

Monte Carlo methods are widely used in derivative pricing. The underlying process is simple: we assumes the underlying to drift at a risk-free rate, and we simulate possible paths of the underlying. The price of a derivatives is then implied from the paths generated. Monte Carlo simulation are particularly useful to price any derivatives with "Counter" feature, such as a Target Range Accrual. In this project I attempt to write a Monte Carlo framework in C#, focusing on (1) making it object oriented, thus reusable for any SDE or products and (2) making it runs in parallel, for the speed. A quick guide is available in this note, and the source code can be found here. As a comparison, we will price an European Option and run a quick comparison.

 

Physics

  • Self-Consistent calculation with adaptive boundary condition for electrons in Silicon Nanostructure.

This project was done in collaboration with Dr G. Gopir. It involves computing the eigenfunction and eigenenergies of electrons in the inversion layer in an n-MOSFET's gate, by solving the Schrodinger and Maxwell equations itteratively. Stable solutions were found with suitable initial guess. The algorithm was further stablized by considering an adaptive boundary condition. The results was subsequently presented in the Regional Conference of Solid State Science and Technology (RCSSST 2001), and it was published in Solid State Science and Technology 20:88-95.

  • Controlling the repulsive Casimir force with optical Kerr effect.

This project was done in collaboration with Prof C.H. Raymond. Ooi. It seeks a theoretical method to control the Casimir force between two parallel plate by varying the intensity of impinging laser. With a good combination of materials, we can see that the Casimir force may be varied between attractive and repulsive, just by changing the intensity of laser. The results were published in Phys. Rev. A 86:062509. This project was also presented in the National Conference on Physics (PERFIK 2012), with several results subsequently published in Sains Malaysiana 42(12)(2013): 1799â€“1803. These results are the basis to my thesis. The (messed up version) of the codes are available on GitHub.
